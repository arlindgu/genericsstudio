Many small business owners think SEO is something for large companies with large budgets. That's a myth. The basics of search engine optimisation are accessible to anyone – and they deliver measurable results even without an agency.

## Why SEO matters for small businesses

Google is where people look when they need something. "Hairdresser near me", "tax advisor Zurich freelancer", "electrician Basel emergency" – these searches happen thousands of times daily. If your business doesn't appear in the results, you don't exist for these people.

A good ranking brings consistent, free enquiries. Unlike paid advertising, which stops the moment you stop paying, SEO builds long-term visibility.

## The basics that actually matter

### 1. The right keywords

Think about what your customers search for – not what you call your services. "Corporate design" might be your term; your customer searches "logo for my company". Use tools like Google's auto-suggest or Keyword Planner to find real search terms.

### 2. Page titles and meta descriptions

Every page on your website has a title (visible in search results and the browser tab) and a meta description (the short text below). These should contain your most important keyword and be written to make people want to click.

### 3. Headings (H1, H2, H3)

Structure your content with clear headings. The H1 on each page should contain the main keyword. Subheadings (H2, H3) help both readers and Google understand the structure.

### 4. Local SEO

For most small businesses, local visibility is the most important. Create a free Google Business Profile, fill it out completely and collect reviews from satisfied customers. This is one of the most effective SEO measures for local businesses.

### 5. Fast, mobile-friendly website

Google measures loading speed and mobile-friendliness and incorporates them into the ranking. A slow or poorly designed mobile site ranks worse – regardless of how good the content is.

### 6. Regular content

Google rewards websites that are regularly updated with new, relevant content. A blog is ideal for this: it gives you the opportunity to address specific search terms and establish yourself as an expert in your field.

## What takes time – and what works quickly

Local SEO (Google Business Profile, local keywords) shows results relatively quickly. General rankings for competitive search terms take months of consistency. Set realistic expectations: SEO is not a sprint, it's a marathon.

## The one thing most people get wrong

SEO is not about tricking Google. It's about answering questions better than anyone else. Write for your readers, not for algorithms. The best SEO strategy is the one that genuinely helps people – Google is very good at recognising that.
